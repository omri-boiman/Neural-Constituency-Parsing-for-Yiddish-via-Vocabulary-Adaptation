7. FROM A GENTLEMAN TO A LADY, REQUESTING
PERMISSION TO CALL.

No. 2281 C—St., Jan. 11th, 91—

MISS VICTORIA DAVIDSON,—Having had the pleasure of
meeting you once at the house of our mutual friend, Mrs
Bowen, I venture to write to request permission to call upon
you at your residence. I have been but a short time a resident
in this city, but your father will, I think, remember Mr. Martin
Krider, of Chicago, who is my uncle.

Trusting that you will pardon the liberty I am taking, and
grant me a position among your gentlemen acquaintances, I am,

Very respectfully,

H. T. KRIDER.
